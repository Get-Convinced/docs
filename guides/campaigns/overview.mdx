## What is campaign mode?

Convinced is a personalized B2B sales widget. Out of the box, it answers questions about your product. **Campaign mode** is the layer that turns it from a generic assistant into a 1:1 outreach experience: each visitor lands on the widget with a `?c=<token>` parameter that resolves to a specific target account, and the agent runs the conversation against intel you (or Convinced) gathered for that account.

When campaign mode is active, the agent's:

- **Opener** mirrors back what the prospect company actually does.
- **Discovery questions** key off the pain points you supplied.
- **Case-study mentions** are restricted to customers you pre-matched to this account.
- **Competitive positioning** is set up around the specific competitor the prospect uses or is evaluating.

Everything is grounded in the **Intel object** — a structured record describing the target. You can supply that record directly (partner-intel mode) or send rep notes and let Convinced synthesize it (auto-enrich mode).

<Note>
The intel itself is just JSON. The agent does not invent facts to fill gaps — fewer fields means a less personalized session, but never a hallucinated one. See [Intel schema](/guides/campaigns/intel-schema) for the field-by-field reference.
</Note>

## How `?c=<token>` works

```
https://app.getconvinced.ai/widget/v2/<org-slug>?c=<token>
```

When the widget boots, it reads `?c=<token>` and asks the Convinced backend for the matching campaign target. If the row exists, is in an active campaign, and has intel ready (`enrichmentStatus` is `done` or `skipped`), the widget enters **campaign mode** and loads the personalized session. If anything is missing — bad token, archived campaign, enrichment still pending — the widget falls back to a generic session.

The `c` parameter (literally the letter `c`, for "campaign") is the **target token**, not the campaign slug. Tokens are derived from the company name (`Acme Logistics` → `acme-logistics`) unless you set them explicitly.

<Tip>
Tokens are **unique per organization**, not per campaign. Two campaigns inside the same Convinced org cannot share a token; the API returns `409 Conflict` if you try. Across different orgs, tokens are independent.
</Tip>

## Campaign vs. target

| Concept | What it is | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Campaign** | A grouping bucket. Tracks intent (`Q2 Logistics Outreach`) and rolls up reporting. Identified by `slug`. | `q2-logistics-outreach` |
| **Target** | One target account inside a campaign. Holds the intel and the widget token. Identified by `token`. | `acme-logistics` |

A campaign can have many targets; a target lives in exactly one campaign. The widget URL only needs the target token (`?c=acme-logistics`) — the campaign is resolved server-side.

## The two integration modes

  <Card title="Partner-intel mode" icon="lock" href="/guides/campaigns/partner-intel">
    You have a structured intel record from your CRM or research team. Push it directly with `enrich: false` — the row is widget-ready as soon as the API returns.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto-enrich mode" icon="sparkles" href="/guides/campaigns/auto-enrich">
    You have free-form rep notes or a microsite link. Send them with `enrich: true` and Convinced runs the AI research pipeline asynchronously (typically 30-90s).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Side-by-side

| | Partner-intel (`enrich: false`) | Auto-enrich (`enrich: true`) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Input** | Structured `intel` object | `rawResearch` text + optional `links` |
| **Sync/async** | Synchronous — ready on response | Asynchronous — poll until `done` |
| **Latency** | Single API call | 30-90s typical |
| **Source of truth** | You | LLM synthesis grounded in your inputs |
| **Best when** | You already have CRM intel mapped to schema | You have rep notes or a microsite, no time to map fields |
| **Initial `enrichmentStatus`** | `skipped` | `pending` |

### Which mode should I use?

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pick partner-intel if...">
    - You already have structured intel in your CRM (Apollo, Salesforce, ZoomInfo, internal warehouse).
    - Your sales ops or research team produces account briefs in a fixed format.
    - You need the widget URL to be live immediately — for example, a real-time hand-off from a chatbot or webhook.
    - You want full control over what the agent says and never want the LLM to add facts you didn't approve.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pick auto-enrich if...">
    - Reps drop free-form notes (Slack threads, call transcripts, raw research) and you want the agent to use them without human cleanup.
    - You have a microsite or landing page URL the rep has shared with the prospect, and you want the conversation grounded in that page.
    - You're driving outbound from public signals (news, funding announcements) and don't have structured intel yet.
    - You're OK with a 30-90 second delay between target creation and the widget being usable.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I mix the two?">
    Not on the same target. A target is either partner-intel (`enrichmentStatus: skipped`) or auto-enriched (`enrichmentStatus: done`). But you can mix freely within the same campaign — some targets coming from your CRM, others from rep notes — and the widget treats them identically once they're widget-ready.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The lifecycle

Every target moves through `enrichmentStatus`:

```
                    POST /api/campaigns/{slug}/targets
                                    │
                  ┌─────────────────┴─────────────────┐
                  │                                   │
            enrich: false                       enrich: true
            (partner-intel)                     (auto-enrich)
                  │                                   │
                  ▼                                   ▼
          ┌─────────────┐                     ┌─────────────┐
          │  skipped    │                     │  pending    │
          │ widgetReady │                     │             │
          └─────────────┘                     └──────┬──────┘
                                                     │
                                              (worker picks up)
                                                     │
                                                     ▼
                                              ┌─────────────┐
                                              │  running    │
                                              └──────┬──────┘
                                                     │
                                       ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
                                       │                           │
                                       ▼                           ▼
                                ┌─────────────┐             ┌─────────────┐
                                │    done     │             │   failed    │
                                │ widgetReady │             │ enrichError │
                                └─────────────┘             └─────────────┘
```

</Frame>

### State reference

<ResponseField name="pending" type="enrichmentStatus">
Auto-enrich job has been queued but the synthesis worker hasn't started yet. The widget falls back to a generic session if the visitor lands now.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="running" type="enrichmentStatus">
Synthesis is in flight: web search, KB lookup, and link crawling are running in parallel, then GPT-5.5 stitches them into the intel object. Typical duration 30-90s.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="done" type="enrichmentStatus">
Auto-enrich completed successfully. `intel` is populated, `enrichedAt` is set, and `widgetReady` flips to `true` (assuming the campaign is still active).
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="failed" type="enrichmentStatus">
Synthesis threw an error. `enrichmentError` is populated (truncated to 500 chars). The row is **not** widget-ready and there are **no automatic retries** — re-PATCH the target to retry. Web-search and KB-lookup failures are non-fatal and do not move the row to `failed`; only synthesis errors do.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="skipped" type="enrichmentStatus">
Partner-intel mode. The intel was supplied directly, no synthesis was needed. `widgetReady` is true immediately.
</ResponseField>

<Tip>
Use the **`widgetReady`** boolean as your gate, not `enrichmentStatus` directly. It encodes all three preconditions: campaign is active, intel is present, and status is `done` or `skipped`. See [Sending visitors to the widget](/guides/campaigns/widget-embedding#widgetready-gate) for the recommended polling pattern.
</Tip>

The dashed boundary is important: target creation and intel synthesis are separate. If you POST a target with `enrich: true`, the API returns immediately (`enrichmentStatus: pending`) and Convinced kicks off the synthesis worker in the background. You poll `GET /api/campaigns/{slug}/targets/{token}` until the status flips.

## Common patterns

### Outbound email campaign

1. Sales ops produces a list of 200 target accounts in CSV.
2. A nightly job loops through the CSV and POSTs one target per row in **partner-intel** mode (intel comes from your CRM).
3. The job emails each prospect a personalized landing page link: `yourcompany.com/demo?c=<token>`.
4. When prospects click, the widget loads in campaign mode and runs the conversation grounded in their record.

### Rep-driven 1:1 outreach

1. The rep finishes account research in Notion / Google Doc.
2. They POST a target in **auto-enrich** mode with `rawResearch` (their notes) + `links` (the prospect's microsite).
3. While the synthesis worker runs (30-90s), the rep drafts the outreach email.
4. Once `widgetReady: true`, the rep includes the personalized link in the email.

### Inbound demo request

1. Visitor fills out a form on your site mentioning their company.
2. Server-side, you POST a target with `enrich: true` and the company name as `rawResearch`.
3. After ~60s, you redirect them to the widget with `?c=<token>` for a personalized demo conversation.
